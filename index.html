Mahatma Gandhi

A protagonist of peace

Here's a timeline of Mahatma gandhi's life :

  • 1869-Birth at Porbandar.
  • 1882-Marriage with Kasturbai, aged 13.
  • 1888-Son, Harilal born.
  • 1888-Reached Southampton, England
  • 1888-Enrolled at the Inner Temple, Inn of Court
  • 1891-Called to the Bar.
  • 1892-Son, Manilal born.
  • 1893-Reached Durban, Natal.
  • 1893-Refused to remove his Paghadi; left the court.
  • 1893-Thrown off the first class carriage of the train at Pietermartizburg Station.
  • 1893-Read Tolstoy's 'Kingdom of God is Within You'.
  • 1894-Founded the 'Natal Indian Congress'.
  • 1896-Commenced writing the 'Green Pamphlet' at Rajkot.
  • 1896-Public lecture at Poona; chaired by Ramkrishna Bhandarkar.
  • 1897-Son, Ramdas born.
  • 1899-Established Indian Ambulance Corp during the Boer War.
  • 1900-Assisted Kasturbai in the delivary of son Devadas.
  • 1903-First issue of 'Indian Opinion' published.
  • 1904-Established 'Phoenix Settlement'.
  • 1906-Took the vow of Brahmcharya.
  • 1906-Advent of Satyagraha at Empire Theatre, Johannesburg, where people took a pledge with God as their witness, to oppose the Black Act.
  • 1906-Left for England as a member of the Indian Delegation.
  • 1907-Asiatic Registration Bill passed in Transvaal Parliament.
  • 1908-The term 'Satyagraha' adopted based on Maganlal Gandhi's fomulation 'Sadagraha'.
  • 1908-Awarded two months' simple imprisonment for refusal to obtain registration.
  • 1908-Agreement signed with General Smuts on voluntary registration.
  • 1908-Mir Alam and others assault Gandhiji.
  • 1908-Breach of faith and bonfire of certificate.
  • 1908-Awarded two months' rigourous imprisonment for entry into the Transvaal without permit.
  • 1909-Left for England as a member of the Indian Delegation.
  • 1909-Wrote 'Hind Swarajya' aboard the ship 'Kildonan Castle'.
  • 1909-'Hind Swarajya' published in 'Indian Opinion'.
  • 1910-'Hind Swarajya' proscribed. Sent a copy of English rendering 'Indian Home Rule' to Tolstoy.
  • 1910-Established Tolstoy Farm; vowed to eschew milk; commenced experiments in frutarian diet.
  • 1912-Gopalkrishna Gokhale's South Africa tour commenced.
  • 1913-Kasturba and others arrested.
  • 1913-Kasturba was awarded 3 months' rigourous imprisonment.
  • 1913-MKG released from jail unconditionally with others, before the completion of the period of imprisonement.
  • 1913-Kasturba released from prison.
  • 1914-Indians' Relief Bill (1914), passed.
  • 1914-Travelled to London to meet Gokhale; Phoenix party at Santiniketan.
  • 1915-Visited Santiniketan.
  • 1915-Visited Kumbh Mela, Hardwar.
  • 1915-Established 'Satyagraha Ashram', Kochrab.
  • 1915-Awarded the 'Kaisare Hind' medal.
  • 1915-First Harijan family of Dudabhai, Danibehn and Laxmi joined the Ashram.
  • 1915-Elected as Vice President of Gujarat Sabha.
  • 1916-Lectured at Benaras Hindu University.
  • 1916-Met Jawaharlal Nehru for the first time at Lucknow Congress.
  • 1917-Travelled to Patna with Rajkumar Shukla to investigate condition of indigo farmers.
  • 1917-Gave a statement in the court about disobedience of prohibitary orders.
  • 1917-Sabarmati Ashram established.
  • 1917-Presided over the Second Gujarati Education Conference, Bharuch.
  • 1917-Presided over the First Gujarat Political Conference, Godhara.
  • 1917-Mahadev Desai joined as Secretary.
  • 1918-Appointed to arbitar the dispute between Mill-owners and Mill-hands of Ahmedabad.
  • 1918-Indefinite fast to keep the resolve of striking Mill-hands commenced.
  • 1918-Broke fast after compromise.
  • 1918-Addressed the public meeting of 5000 peasants at Nadiad; advised them to not pay land revenue.
  • 1919-Satyagraha pledge against 'Rowlett Act'.
  • 1919-National Hartal and Satyagraha against the Rowlett Act.
  • 1919-Arrested at Palval Station.
  • 1919-Massacre at Jallianwala Baug; three days' fast in Ahmedabad commenced.
  • 1919-First issue of 'Navajivan' published.
  • 1919-First issue of 'Young India' published.
  • 1919-Congress boycott the Hunter Commission and set up independent inquiry.
  • 1920-Returned the honours to the Government, commencement of Non-co-operation.
  • 1920-Gujarat Vidyapith established.
  • 1921-Given the reigns of the Congress in Ahmedabad.
  • 1922-Bardoli Taluka resolved against payment of land revenue and Civil-Disobedience.
  • 1922-Policemen at Chauri Chaura killed.
  • 1922-Gandhiji and Shankerlal Banker arrested.
  • 1922-Charged of sedition for the authorship of three articles in Young India.
  • 1922-Trial held at Ahmedabad Circuit House; Judge Broomfield awarded Gandhiji a sentence of six years' imprisonment.
  • 1922-Transferred to Yeravda Prison.
  • 1924-Operated for appendicites at Sasoon Hospital, Poona.
  • 1924-Released unconditionally.
  • 1924-The publication of 'Dakshin Africana Satyagrahano Itihas' in Navajivan.
  • 1924-Twenty one days' fast for communal harmony commenced at Maulana Mohammed Ali's house, Delhi.
  • 1924-Presided over the Belgaum Congress.
  • 1925-'All India Spinners' Association' established.
  • 1925-Madeleine Slade joined Satyagraha Ashram, named Mirabehn.
  • 1925-The publication of 'Satyana Prayogo athava Atmakatha' commences in Navajivan.
  • 1925-The publication of 'Autobiography or The Story of My Experiments with Truth' commences in Young India.
  • 1928-Boycott of Simon Commission.
  • 1929-Wrote introduction to 'Anasktiyoga'.
  • 1929-Viceroy Lord Irwin annouces Round Table Conference.
  • 1929-Purna Swaraj Resolution adopted at Lahore Congress.
  • 1930-Wrote to the Viceroy about his intention to break the Salt Law.
  • 1930-Dandi March commences from Satyagraha Ashram.
  • 1930-Broke the Salt Law.
  • 1930-Arrested and sent to Yeravda Prison.
  • 1931-Released from prison.
  • 1931-Gandhi-Irwin pact signed.
  • 1931-In London as the sole representative of the Congress at the Second Round Table Conference.
  • 1931-Recorded a message for the people of America.
  • 1931-Met Maria Montessori.
  • 1931-'God is' recorded.
  • 1931-Attended (in his loin-cloth) the Royal reception for the delegates of the Second Round Table Conference at the Buckingham Palace at the invitation of Emperor George V.
  • 1931-Declared his opposition to the proposed Communal Award.
  • 1931-Second Round Table Conference ends.
  • 1931-With Romain Rolland.
  • 1931-In Vatican. Met Mussolini in Rome.
  • 1932-Congress Working Committee adopted the resolution of Civil Disobedience.
  • 1932-Arrested and sent to Yeravda for indefinite period; Vallabhbhai fellow prisoner.
  • 1932-Mahadev Desai transfered to Yeravda.
  • 1932-Premier Ramsay Macdonald announced the Communal Award.
  • 1932-Letter to Ramsay Macdonald declaring his intentions to fast unto death against the Communal Award.
  • 1932-The fast commenced.
  • 1932-Poona Pact signed.
  • 1932-Broke the fast.
  • 1932-'Harijan Sevak Sangh' established.
  • 1933-Commneced publication of 'Harijan'. 'Harijan Sevak' (Hindi), commenced publication on 23rd February and 'Harijanbandhu' (Gujarati) commenced publication on 12th March.
  • 1933-Resolved to fast for 21 days at midnight.
  • 1933-Issued a statement about the fast; unconditional and for self-purification.
  • 1933-Commenced the fast. Released from prison.
  • 1933-Fast broken.
  • 1933-Declare his intention to offer Individual Satyagraha.
  • 1933-Arrested at Ahmedabad and sent to Sabarmati Prison; shifted to Yeravda later.
  • 1933-Went to Wardha with a decision to not involve himself with politics.
  • 1933-Satyagraha Ashram, Sabarmati given to Harijan Sevak Sangh.
  • 1933-Nationwide Harijan Yatra commenced.
  • 1934-A mob attacked in Bihar at the instigation of Lalanath Shastri.
  • 1934-Toured Orissa on foot.
  • 1934-Withdrew Collective Satyagraha.
  • 1934-Bomb thrown at his motorcade in Poona.
  • 1934-Declared his intention to retire from Congress.
  • 1934-Resigned from the Congress.
  • 1934-'All India Village Industries' Association' established.
  • 1936-Moved to Segaon from Wardha.
  • 1936-Presided over the 12th Gujarati Sahitya Parishad.
  • 1936-Travancore temples opened to Harijans.
  • 1939-Kasturba arrested in Rajkot by Thakoresaheb.
  • 1939-Commenced fast at Rajkot against the breach of trust.
  • 1939-Broke the fast; Chief Justice Maurice Gwyer appointed the arbitrator.
  • 1939-Sir Maurice Gwyer declared award in favour of the people of Rajkot; Muslims and Bhayats demonstrate against Gandhiji and disrupt a prayer meeting.
  • 1939-Wrote a letter to Adolf Hitler.
  • 1939-Congress Ministries resign.
  • 1940-Segaon named Sevagram.
  • 1940-Congress Working Committee meeting at Sevagram; Individual Satyagraha proposed.
  • 1940-Vinoba chosen as the first Individual Satyagrahi.
  • 1941-'On Constructive Programme' published.
  • 1941-The Congress Working Committee at Bardoli accepted Gandhiji's request to free him from the responsibility of guiding the Congress.
  • 1942-Informed Congress Working Committee at Sevagram that Jawaharlal Nehru would be his political heir.
  • 1942-Advised the Cripps Mission to return to England.
  • 1942-Congress Working Committee adopted the resolution for immediate withdrawl of the British from India.
  • 1942-'Quit India' resolution adopted; gave the call 'Do or Die'.
  • 1942-Gandhiji and Congress Working Committee members arrested; Gandhiji imprisoned at the Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1942-Death of Mahadev Desai;cremated at Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1943-Commenced a 21 day fast.
  • 1944-Death of Kasturba; cremated at Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1944-Released unconditionally .
  • 1945-Lord Wavell calls for negotiations.
  • 1945-Congress Working Committee members released from Ahmedanagar Fort Prison. Ban on Congress lifted.
  • 1945-The Viceroy declared the Simla Conference a failure.
  • 1946-Three member British delegation reaches Delhi.
  • 1946-The Congress Working Committee adopted resolution to accept the formation of the Constituent Assembly.
  • 1946-The Viceroy formed an Interim Government.
  • 1946-Communal riots in Calcutta.
  • 1946-A 12 member Interim Government headed by Pandit Nehru formed.
  • 1946-Communal violence in Noakhali.
  • 1946-Muslim League representatives joined the Interim Government.
  • 1946-Communal riots in Bihar.
  • 1946-Left for Noakhali.
  • 1946-Handed over the responsibility of Harijan Journals to Kaka Kalelkar, Kishorelal Mashruwala and Narahari Parikh.
  • 1947-Bare Foot March to Noakhali commences.
  • 1947-Tour of violence-affected villages of Bihar.
  • 1947-Met the new Viceroy Lord Mountbatten.
  • 1947-Addressed the Asian Relations Conference.
  • 1947-Left for Bihar.
  • 1947-The Congress, the Muslim League and the representatives of the Sikhs accept the plan of Partition.
  • 1947-The Congress Working Committee adopted resolution to accept the Partition.
  • 1947-In Calcutta with Shaheed Suhrawardy
  • 1947-Spent the day in fast and prayer.
  • 1947-Indefinite fast against the communal violence in Calcutta.
  • 1947-Broke the fast.
  • 1947-Reached Delhi.
  • 1948-Commenced fast at Delhi.
  • 1948-Broke the fast.
  • 1948-A bomb thrown during the Prayer Meeting.
  • 1948-Took three bullets on his chest. Godse the assassin.
  • 1948-Cremated on the banks of the Yamuna by Ramdas.

"You may never know what results come of your actions, but if you do nothing, there will be no results."

-- Mahatma Gandhi
